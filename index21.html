Modern English teaching trends in modern coursebooks
Communicative language teaching has come a long way. At its heart, it is still an approach where the ultimate goal is to help our students communicate effectively. But there is now a lot of choice about how to go about meeting that objective.
This variety of choice is reflected in the decisions coursebook writers have made about the materials and activities they include in their books.
During the webinar, we will focus on what modern teaching trends look like when expressed in published materials. Hopefully, this will help us to get the most out of them, select the best ones for our context, or even design our own.
Anastasia
Dereviankina
15:15 - 16:45
The importance of soft skills for students
In language teaching we often focus on language hard skills. Grammar, grammatical accuracy, having a wide vocabulary, getting the spelling right, being able to pronounce ‘ship’ and ‘sheep’ differently, and so on.
But then we have soft skills - the ability to relate to and interact successfully with other people. Aren’t they also necessary for successful communication?
In this session we will talk more about what these soft skills are, why they are necessary for language learning, how a lack of them can impact successful language acquisition, and how we can help students get better at them.

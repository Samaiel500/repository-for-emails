Modern Language Classroom Trends. Effective Teaching and Learning
The students come to our classes with certain expectations and many of them are experienced learners, which means we need to use modern, varied techniques, tailored directly to them. This session aims to answer a number of questions: What are the criteria of a modern and effective lesson? and How do we apply them in our teaching? TEFL authors claim we live in a post-approach era and the way we teach is eclectic, but what makes it ‘informed’? We’ll talk about different language focused lesson frameworks and the choices we make while planning our lessons for a particular group of students.

Mediating authenticity or integrating skills?
We are all familiar with the Council of Europe’s Frame of Reference for language learners, or the CEFR scale, as a way of describing students’ level. A1 for beginners through C2 for proficiency and everything in between.However, as we know, the scales are not based on what grammatical structures the students have mastered or the number of words they know, but on what they can do in English.One of the skills the CEFR scale explores is mediation. But what does that mean? In this session we will discuss this, and show how it is related to the use of authentic texts and even more to authentic tasks in class, helping us to update the way we run an integrated skills lesson.
